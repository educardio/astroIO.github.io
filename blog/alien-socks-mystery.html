Hello, fellow earthlings, and welcome back to the blog that tackles the truly unimportant questions of the universe! Today, we're diving headfirst into a cosmic quandary that has kept precisely zero scientists up at night: Where do aliens keep their socks?

Now, before you click away and go back to watching cat videos (a very rational choice), hear me out. If aliens are visiting us, buzzing our air traffic control towers, and maybe even stealing our crops (for a very specific salad recipe, I assume), they must have laundary. And if they have laundry, they must have socks.

This, my friends, is a problem of universal scale.

The Leading (and Utterly Ridiculous) Theories

After weeks of rigorous research (read: staring at the ceiling and eating stale chips), I've developed a few high-level, low-IQ hypotheses.

  • 1. The Dimensional Pocket Theory (The "TARDIS" Drawer)

    • The Idea: Their socks are stored in a tiny, non-Euclidean compartment. It's bigger on the inside, connected to a pocket dimension where the speed of light is exactly the same as the speed of putting on a sock.
    • The Problem: If their storage is that advanced, why do they still only have one sock left in the pair? Some mysteries transcend technology.
